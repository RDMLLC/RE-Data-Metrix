Types of Loans for Rental Property Investors
Conventional Loans
Down Payment: 15–25%
Credit Score: 620–700+
Standard fixed-rate or adjustable-rate options for investment properties.
Button: Find Conventional Lender
DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio) Loans
Down Payment: 20–35%
Credit Score: 620–680+
Qualification based on property cash flow rather than personal income.
Overlaps with conventional loans on down payment and credit requirements.
Shares property-based underwriting with hard money loans but offers longer terms and lower rates.
	Button: Find DSCR Lender
Hard Money Loans
Down Payment: 10–40%
Credit Score: Flexible, often no strict minimum (550+ preferred)
Short-term, asset-focused loans for fix-and-flip or quick financing.
Buy a distressed property with a hard money (bridge) loan, fix it up, refi out of it with a DSCR loan.

Button: Find Bridge Lenders

FHA and VA Loans
FHA Down Payment: 3.5–10% (owner-occupied multi-family)
VA Down Payment: 0% (for veterans, owner-occupied)
Credit Scores: 500–620+ depending on loan type.
Typically require owner occupancy; not standard for pure investment.

Button: Find FHA/VA Lenders


Portfolio and Blanket Loans
Down Payment: 20–30%
Credit Score: Varies, typically 680+
Used for multiple properties under one loan.

5/1 ARM Loans (Adjustable-Rate Mortgage)
Fixed interest rate for the first 5 years, then annual adjustments.
Down Payment: Usually around 5% minimum.
Credit Score: Typically 620+ required.
Good for investors planning to hold property ≤5 years to benefit from lower initial rates.
Requires ability to handle potential payment increases after adjustment period.

Button: Find 5/1 ARM Lenders


Balloon Loans
Feature smaller periodic payments with a large lump sum due at loan end (commonly after 5–7 years).
Down Payment and Credit: Similar to conventional loans (20%+; good credit 620+).
Often used when investors plan to refinance or sell before balloon payment is due.

Button: Find Balloon Loan Lenders

Interest-Only Loans
Borrower pays only interest for the initial period (e.g., 5–10 years), then principal plus interest after.
Down Payment: Often 20% or higher.
Credit: Usually requires solid credit (680+).
Useful for maximizing cash flow early in ownership with expectation of property appreciation or refinance.

Button: Find Interest-Only Lenders
Overlaps and Considerations
DSCR loans overlap most with conventional loans in down payment and credit criteria but differ by focusing on rental income for approval.
5/1 ARM loans and balloon loans are often structured as conventional loans but offer flexible payment schedules suited to shorter-term investment strategies.
Interest-only loans can be conventional or specialized loans with cash flow and payment flexibility.
Hard money loans and DSCR loans share leniency on income documentation but differ markedly in term length and cost.
Choosing among these depends on investment goals (short vs. long term), credit profile, and tolerance for payment variability.
